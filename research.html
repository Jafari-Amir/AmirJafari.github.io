2.0 / Research

Research at the interface of behavior, neural signals, and quantitative analysis.

My research interests are concentrated around behavioral neuroscience, addiction-related behavior, EEG/ERP, biological time series, and computational interpretation of experimental data.

2.1 / Behavioral neuroscience

I study behavioral phenotypes related to addiction, anxiety-like behavior, reward processing, developmental exposure, and treatment-related behavioral changes.

My animal behavior background includes experimental design, behavioral tracking, and interpretation of behavioral outcomes in relation to pharmacological and developmental factors.

2.2 / EEG and signal analysis

I am interested in high-density EEG, ERP paradigms, oddball tasks, and how neural signals change in response to treatment, condition, or experimental context.

This part of my work connects neuroscience with time-series analysis, signal processing, and careful statistical interpretation.

2.3 / Computational analysis

I use quantitative reasoning to study biological data, including Python-based workflows, statistical modeling, dimensionality reduction, and machine-learning-oriented analysis.

The aim is not only prediction, but defensible biological interpretation.
